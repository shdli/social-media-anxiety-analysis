# Digital  Wellbeing & Mental Health Analysis
## Project Overview
This project investigates the relationship between digital usage habits and mental wellbeing. By analyzing a dataset of over 6,000 participants, we aim to uncover how screen time, notifications, and sleep patterns correlate with anxiety levels, and whether behavioral interventions like digital detox or screen limits effectively mitigate stress.

## Technologies Used
* **Python**
* **Pandas** (for data analysis)
* **Matplotlib / Seaborn** (for data visualization)
* **Jupyter Notebook**

## Key Findings
* **Strong Correlation between Screen Time and Anxiety:** Our analysis revealed a clear positive correlation between high daily screen hours/notifications and increased anxiety scores, suggesting that digital intensity is a primary stressor.
* **Consistency vs. Intervention:** Interestingly, the data suggests that "quick-fix" interventions like digital detox attempts do not significantly lower anxiety. Instead, sustained digital hygiene and healthy lifestyle choices (such as regular physical activity) appear to be more effective buffers against digital-induced stress.
* **Psychological Drivers:** Factors such as FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) and social comparison were identified as significant psychological mediators that bridge the gap between heavy screen usage and poor mental wellbeing.
